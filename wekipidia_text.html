CHEMISTRY is the scientific study of propertise and behaviour of matter. [1][2]It is physical sciencewithin the natural sciences that studies the chemical elements that make up matter and compounds made of atom , molecules and ions:their composition , structure , properties, behaviour and the changes they undergo during reactions with other substances.[3][4][5][6] Chemistry also addresses the nature ofchemical bonds in chemical compounds.
In the scope of its subject, chemistry occupies an intermidiate position between physics and biology. [7] It is sometimes called the central science because it provides a foundation for understanding both basic and applied scientific discipline at a fundamental level. [8] For example, chemistry explains aspects of plant growth (botany), the formation of igeous rocks (geology), how atmospheric ozone is formed and how enviromental pollutants are degraded (ecology), the propertise of the soil on the Moon (cosmochemistry), how medicines work (pharmacology), and how to collect DNA evidence at a crime scene (forensics).[9]
